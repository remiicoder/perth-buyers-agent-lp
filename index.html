How it works

Four steps. One outcome

  1. 01

    We talk

    Free, no pitch. I want to know what you're actually looking for — the commute, the school zone, the street you'd say no to regardless. And I want to know what's gone wrong. Most searches aren't stuck because of budget. They're stuck because the strategy is off.

  2. 02

    I go looking

    I talk to agents in the inner south, bayside and western suburbs daily. I've become someone they call before a listing goes live. You'll see properties other buyers don't know exist yet — and I'll bring you two or three worth your time, not twenty.

  3. 03

    We check everything

    Before any offer goes in: building and pest, flood overlays, comparable sales, so you know what the property is actually worth.

  4. 04

    I negotiate

    I've bought well below comparable sales many times. The gap between what a property sells for and what it could have sold for often comes down to who's doing the talking. I handle auctions and private treaty. You don't have to be in the room.

Can't I just negotiate myself?

You could, but it's risky... as the person on the other side does this every week, and their job is to get the highest price for someone else. I assure you'll never overpay buying a property, when I negotiate on your behalf.

What does it cost?

We charge a small percentage of the final purchase price. You'll know exactly what you're paying — regardless of what the property costs — in advance.

How long does a search take?

My average is 6 to 8 weeks. Without a buyer's agent, the typical southern Brisbane buyer searches 9 to 18 months.

About 40% of my clients are investors, most of whom buy from interstate.
